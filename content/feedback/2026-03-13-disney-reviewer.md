# Resume Review Feedback - Disney Contact (2026-03-13)

## Questions from Reviewer
- What type of roles are you interviewing for? What engineering level? (P2, P3 (Senior), P4 (Lead))
- What details do you have about the roles?

## What to Expect (Disney Interview Process)
- If applying through a recruiter, they can get you into consideration for interview
- 4 or 5 rounds of interviews when making it all the way, ~60 minutes each
- Hiring manager may do first interview, then meet with leads (1-2 interviewers per session)
- Sessions include behavioral questions and code challenges
- Be prepared to talk about **scale** (e.g., building a web API with mass amounts of data - too much to load at once)

## General Observations
- Layout looks good
- **Shift emphasis** from "AI-First Engineering" to "Full Stack Engineer: AI and Web Applications"
  - Most engineers at Disney are not AI-only
  - On web app teams: need to show you can do the job without AI, plus AI skills for efficiency
  - On AI teams: expected to know data science deep end (algorithms, models, training)
- **Emphasize AWS, Docker, Kubernetes** for web app roles
  - System Engineers handle heavy lifting, but showing experience is good
  - Highlight: API Gateway, Lambda (serverless), S3, MongoDB Atlas, messaging, queues

## Experience Format Feedback
- **Lead with business value**: "Saved the company X amount of dollars using Y tools that delivered Z..."
  - Work enables company to: make money, cut costs, improve velocity, decrease friction, expand growth
- **Value first, tools second**: Lead bullets with value delivered, then support with tools used
- **Emphasize teamwork**: Show how you deliver solutions with people you worked with
  - Working solo may be a red flag — expected to work well in group settings
- **Trim older experience**: Focus on most recent roles. Less is more with focus on impact.
